ANGAT BUHAY

Angat Buhay is a non-government organization (NGO) in the Philippines that aims to uplift the lives of marginalized communities through collaborative and sustainable solutions. Launched after the term of former Vice President Leni Robredo, Angat Buhay continues the legacy of her anti-poverty initiatives by fostering partnerships with various sectors, including private organizations, civil society groups, and local government units. The organization’s mission is rooted in the belief that collective action and compassion can drive meaningful change, especially for the most vulnerable Filipinos.

Central to Angat Buhay’s approach is its focus on key areas that address the root causes of poverty. In education, the organization provides scholarships, builds classrooms, and supplies learning materials to ensure that underprivileged children have access to quality education. Recognizing the importance of health and nutrition, Angat Buhay also implements medical missions, feeding programs, and health-related projects that cater to underserved communities.

Angat Buhay also believes in the power of the youth and volunteerism. The organization actively engages young people and volunteers in its projects, fostering a culture of empathy, service, and civic responsibility. By providing platforms for volunteer engagement, Angat Buhay ensures that more Filipinos can take part in nation-building efforts. Through its comprehensive programs and partnership-driven approach, Angat Buhay continues to make a significant impact on the lives of many Filipinos. The organization’s work demonstrates that through unity, compassion, and action, it is possible to create a society where every Filipino can live with dignity and hope.
